Skutla privacy policy

Effective 1 May 2026
Skutla is a decentralized peer-to-peer ride-matching demo. It runs entirely on your device and exchanges messages directly with other users through a free public MQTT broker. There is no Skutla-operated server, account system, or analytics. The data you enter stays on your device and is shared only with other Skutla peers when you actively use the app.

Who runs Skutla

Skutla is an independent open-source hobby project. It is not affiliated with or operated by any company or organization. Source code is at github.com/sigmarkarl/skutla. Questions or concerns can be raised by opening an issue at github.com/sigmarkarl/skutla/issues.

What information the app handles

The app processes the following data, only while you are using it:

  • Peer ID — a random UUID generated on first launch. Not tied to your name, phone, or email.
  • Display name — whatever you type when picking a role.
  • Role — Driver or Passenger.
  • Location — your device GPS coordinates, used to find nearby peers and to render the map. Only read while the app is in the foreground.
  • Driver-only optional data — vehicle make/model/color/plate; contact (phone, WhatsApp, Messenger); payment handles (Aur phone, PayPal, Venmo, WeChat ID); preferred currency. You enter these yourself and they are sent only to riders you bid on, and only when the "Share contact" toggle is on.
  • Trip data — pickup and destination coordinates, agreed price, start/end time. Used to coordinate the ride and shown in your local ride history.
  • Ratings — 1-5 star scores you receive from past counterparts. Stored locally; an aggregate average is broadcast with your presence so others can see it.

Where the data goes

  • Locally on your device, in your browser's localStorage (web) or shared_preferences (iOS/Android). Clearing the app's storage / browser data permanently deletes everything.
  • Through a public MQTT broker at broker.hivemq.com, run by HiveMQ GmbH as a free public service. Messages travel over TLS WebSockets. The broker may store retained presence messages for short periods to deliver them to subscribers. Skutla does not have an account or contractual relationship with HiveMQ. See HiveMQ's privacy policy.
  • To OpenStreetMap for map tiles (tile.openstreetmap.org) and reverse geocoding to pick a currency (nominatim.openstreetmap.org). Your coordinates are sent to OSM whenever a tile or geocode is requested. See OSMF's privacy policy.
  • To other Skutla peers in your geohash cell — your live location, display name, role, plus any optional driver info above — while you are running the app.

What we do not collect

  • No analytics, no telemetry, no crash reporting, no advertising IDs.
  • No account creation, password, email, phone number, or government ID requested by the app itself.
  • No backend logs — Skutla operates no server.

Children

Skutla is not intended for children under 13. Don't use it if you are.

Your rights

Because Skutla doesn't run a backend, "delete my account" simply means clearing the app's local storage on your device:

  • Web: clear site data for the Skutla domain in your browser settings.
  • iOS/Android: uninstall the app, or use the OS storage controls to clear app data.

Once cleared, your peer ID is regenerated on next launch and previous data cannot be recovered.
